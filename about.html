Hi. My name is Toni Tran, and I’m a software engineer who specializes in product aesthetics and user experience.

Design, for me, is more than just the pure look of a product. It’s about the experience and core values. What the users can see and interact with is the ultimate bridge to connect between machines and humanity. Machines are created to deliver magical services that undeniably enhance our life, and play a large influence on social development. Nevertheless, none of these can be achieved if the learning curve is way too steep, or the efforts and time required to master are way too undesirable. As a software engineer and designer, I believe my goal is to find the most optimal solution to fill this gap, making the product as simple to use as possible, yet still gives incredibly enjoyable experience as well as high level of professionalism.

In order to build the most perfected product, I think asking good questions is an indispensable practice. Good questions are the source motivations in any kind of work, steering me in the right direction, reminding me why I’m here in the first place. Good questions are what light up and lead to remarkable achievement driving our society forward, conquering the most critical challenges in a complicated world.

I see myself as a quite person, who enjoys to keep my heads down and focus on the work. Nevertheless, I love to spend time listening to people sharing their ideas, opinions and stories. One of the things I’ve learned is the importance of listening because the very best ideas can very often come from the quietest voice. I perceive committed collaboration and trust among teammates or partners to be imperative factors in order to do any great work.

My dream is to make a positive impact on people’s lives all over the globe through technology. This is a long road, but I believe with small progresses, starting with helping people around me or resolving the most mundane needs in my community, will eventually come a big change.
